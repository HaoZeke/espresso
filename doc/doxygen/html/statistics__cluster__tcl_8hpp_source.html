25 /** Parser for the necklace cluster algorithm
26 
27  \verbatim analyze necklace <pearl_treshold> <back_dist> <space_dist> <first> <length> \endverbatim
28 
29  For more information see: Limbach H.J. and Holm C. Single-Chain
30  Properties of Polyelectrolytes in Poor Solvent J. Phys. Chem. B,
31  107 (32), 8041 -8055, AUG 14 2003. The first three parameters are
32  tune parameters for the algorithm: pearl_treshold is the minimal
33  number of monomers in a pearl. back_dist is the number of monomers
34  along the chain backbone which are excluded from the space
35  distance criterion to form clusters. space_dist is the distance
36  between two monomers up to which they are considered to belong to
37  the same clusters. The three parameters may be connected by
38  scaling arguments. Make sure that your results are only weakly
39  dependent on the exact choice of your parameters. For the
40  algorithm the coordinates stored in \ref partCfg are used. The
41  chain itself is defined by the identity first of its first monomer
42  and the chain length length.
43 */
44 int tclcommand_analyze_parse_necklace(Tcl_Interp *interp, int argc, char **argv);
