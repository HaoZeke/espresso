23 /** \file p3m-common.hpp common functions for dipolar and charge p3m.
24  *
25  * We use here a P3M (Particle-Particle Particle-Mesh) method based
26  * on the Ewald summation. Details of the used method can be found in
27  * Hockney/Eastwood and Deserno/Holm. The file p3m contains only the
28  * Particle-Mesh part.
29  *
30  * Further reading:
31  * <ul>
32  * <li> P.P. Ewald,
33  * <i>Die Berechnung optischer und elektrostatischer Gitterpotentiale</i>,
34  * Ann. Phys. (64) 253-287, 1921
35  * <li> R. W. Hockney and J. W. Eastwood,
36  * <i>Computer Simulation Using Particles</i>,
37  * IOP, London, 1988
38  * <li> M. Deserno and C. Holm,
39  * <i>How to mesh up {E}wald sums. I. + II.</i>,
40  * J. Chem. Phys. (109) 7678, 1998; (109) 7694, 1998
41  * <li> M. Deserno, C. Holm and H. J. Limbach,
42  * <i>How to mesh up {E}wald sums. </i>,
43  * in Molecular Dynamics on Parallel Computers,
44  * Ed. R. Esser et al., World Scientific, Singapore, 2000
45  * <li> M. Deserno,
46  * <i>Counterion condensation for rigid linear polyelectrolytes</i>,
47  * PhdThesis, Universit{\"a}t Mainz, 2000
48  * <li> J.J. Cerda, P3M for dipolar interactions. J. Chem. Phys, 129, xxx ,(2008).
49  * </ul>
50  *
51  */
